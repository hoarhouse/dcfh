Why We Exist

In the age of artificial intelligence, the very definition of what it means to be human is being rewritten. We are called to help chart a course for humanity where technology—one of the highest and best expressions of our collective talents, hopes, and innovations—is advanced in authentic service of the human family.

Like every advancement of the human spirit, AI can be an incredible gift to improve the lives of billions and to find viable solutions to urgent environmental, food security, education, and other global challenges. Conversely, it can also accelerate inequality and discrimination, eroding the human and ethical dimension from our societies.

AI is increasingly present in every aspect of daily life. The multitudinous asymmetries brought about by the digital age—by which a select few know everything about us, while we know nothing about them—dulls societal and democratic engagement, critical thought, and conscious decision-making.

DCF created an international platform that serves as a tool at the service of the Catholic Church in advancing its mission on ethical technology and serving as a beacon of the message that technology can and must lift up humanity, and humanity must not become subjugated to technology.

Our work includes digital humanitarianism to address the digital, information and intelligence divides; ethical AI, cybersecurity, and cyber safety mentoring and curriculum for children and young people; the development of national and international norms to support ethical technology development and use; inter-faith and interdisciplinary programming to develop common efforts in ensuring human dignity is at the core of our technologies; and ultimately to support the Catholic Church in considering and developing a Cyber Authority, as established in other states, and Sovereign AI as a means of preserving and advancing culture, morals, and faith traditions.

We are building a constellation of organizations and individuals with globally recognized expertise in faith, ethics, technology, and human behavior, with the ultimate goal of creating a "cyber commons" as a bridge for ethical, responsible and human centered, value-based cyber and AI technology inspired by Catholic Social teachings and the principles shared across all faiths for humankind.

How We Work

Global Independent Foundation

Established with inspiration from the Catholic Church and Vatican engagement, ensuring unbiased dedication to human-centered ethical technology development through a global nonprofit constellation.

Constellation Design

DCF is a constellation of nonprofits in key geographical areas throughout the Global North, South, East and West to bring together strengths from around the world, provide benefits to everyone everywhere, connect people throughout the world, and avoid geographical tech dominance.

Human-Centric Ethical Technologies

Promoting and supporting cyber technologies like AI, AGI, and cyber safety that prioritize human safety, ethics, and security in development and application.

Transformative Technologies

Dedicated to advancing AI sovereignty, data sovereignty platforms, and cyber safety to shape a secure digital future.

Peace and Common Good

Aligning technology efforts with Catholic Social Teaching and principles shared across other religions and philosophies in service of human dignity to preserve peace and advance the welfare of all humankind.

The Digital Commons

Connect and bring together individuals, experts, technologists, academicians, researchers, governments and regulators, faith leaders, students and programmers, and humanitarian organizations to work together in a commons to advance ethical, moral, value-based, human-centered, secure and safe technologies and cyberspace for humankind. DCF's model provides an advanced web portal to create the digital commons by providing a portal to connect to each other for sharing, recruiting, and participating in each category of the commons.
