Domus Communis Foundation

Building the Future of Ethical AI

We work collectively to promote the ethical and responsible development, deployment, and use of emerging technology in service of the whole human family in the digital age.

Our Mission & Vision

DCF Hungary stands at the crossroads where science, technology, faith, and the future of artificial intelligence converge, ensuring that technology serves humanity rather than the reverse.

Mission

To prioritize human values, ethics, and morality in the design, deployment, and responsible use of technology, ensuring that innovation serves the needs of all people, inspired by Catholic Social Teaching and principles shared across all world religions.

Vision

To build a cyber commons through a global constellation of faith leaders, experts, technologists, academics, and organizations working to advance ethical, moral, value-based, secure and safe cyber technologies and AI for humankind.

Values

Human dignity, equality between every person, and the common good of all humanity guide our work. We partner with and serve all people, regardless of their religion, background, or beliefs.

"AI must be ordered to the human person and become part of efforts to achieve greater justice, more extensive fraternity and a more humane order of social relations, which are more valuable than advances in the technical field."

— Pope Francis, World Economic Forum, January 2025

Catholic Social Teaching Principles

Our work is grounded in the timeless wisdom of Catholic Social Teaching, providing a framework for ethical technology development that serves the common good.

Human Dignity

Every person has inherent worth and must be at the center of technological development, not subjugated to it.

Common Good

Technology should benefit all of humanity, creating social conditions that allow every person to thrive.

Subsidiarity

Decisions should be made at the most local level possible, empowering communities and individuals.

Solidarity

We are all interconnected and responsible for one another's wellbeing in the digital age.

Preferential Option for the Poor and Vulnerable, Dignity of Work and the Rights of Workers

Technology should be leveraged to bridge the gap between the rich and the poor, ensuring that the most vulnerable have access to the tools and opportunities needed to thrive. In the age of automation and digital transformation, technology must be used to enhance the dignity of work, ensuring fair wages, safe working conditions, and the protection of workers' rights in all industries. Access to digitality is paramount to the dignity of children and youth's education.

Care for Creation

Technology must be harnessed responsibly to protect the environment, promoting sustainable innovations that preserve the earth's resources for future generations, aligning with our duty as stewards of God's creation.

Why Hungary?

Hungary stands at a unique crossroads, bridging Global North, South, East, and West, with a rich intellectual tradition and deep faith heritage perfectly positioned to lead ethical AI development.

Scientific Legacy

Hungary has produced scientific luminaries like John von Neumann, pioneer of quantum physics, game theory, and computing. This rich intellectual tradition continues today in AI and cyber research.

Cultural Bridge

Hungary's diverse history positions it as a natural connector between East and West, North and South, making it an ideal hub for global collaboration on ethical technology.

Faith Foundation

Hungary's deep Christian roots and respect for all religious faiths align perfectly with Catholic Social Teaching principles that guide our ethical AI framework.

Our Journey

From inspired vision to global impact - the evolution of DCF's mission to ensure technology serves humanity.

2017
Foundation of Vision
The Dicastery of Integral Human Development symposium 'Prospects for a world free from nuclear weapons and integral disarmament' laid the groundwork for DCF's mission.
2020-22
DCF Formation
Domus Communis Foundation is conceived from the Integral Security Paradigm developed by the Catholic Social Doctrine, to advance ethical technology development globally.
2023
Global Expansion
DCF constellation model launched with operations in USA, Hungary, and Ghana, creating a global network for ethical AI development.
2024
Papal Recognition
Pope Francis's G7 address and subsequent statements reinforce DCF's mission to center human dignity in AI development.
2025
DCF Hungary Launch
Official launch of DCF Hungary as a nonprofit public benefit organization, establishing Central Europe's hub for ethical AI research and development.
