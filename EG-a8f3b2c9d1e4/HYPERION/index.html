The Problem

Photonic computing is coming. Lightmatter just raised $400M at $4.4B valuation. Q.ANT shipped the first commercial photonic processor. MIT demonstrated 200-layer deep learning on photonic chips. But there's a critical gap:
  • No sovereign identity layer exists for photonic AI systems
  • Government and defense buyers require EU-compliant identity verification for classified workloads
  • Photonic compute nodes have no standardized authentication or attestation
  • EU AI Act mandates traceability and accountability ‚Äî impossible without verified identity
  • Current photonic hardware vendors (Lightmatter, Q.ANT, Lumai, Xanadu) are focused purely on chips, not governance software

The Identity Crisis:

  • How does a photonic node prove it is what it claims to be?
  • How do you verify which organization owns/operates a photonic cluster?
  • How do you authenticate federated AI training across hybrid electronic-photonic infrastructure?
  • How do you meet EU data sovereignty requirements when compute happens at the speed of light?

The Solution

HYPERION provides the sovereign identity and trust layer that photonic AI infrastructure requires but doesn't have.
