Ending Digital Warfare, Building Digital & AI Peace

War has traditionally been fought on physical battlegrounds with soldiers, mechanized equipment and armament. The dangers of technology entered the battlefield in World War II with nuclear weapons. In the mid-2010s, a military artificial intelligence arms race began with lethal autonomous weaponry systems. This summit convenes 40 carefully selected global leaders to work together to prevent and cease the use of digital and AI autonomous weapons, artificial superintelligence in weapons, and cyber warfare. With rapidly advancing digital & AI technologies, a peace treaty alone is no longer enough. To attain complete and sustainable integral peace, solutions in both the physical and digital & AI dimensions are required.

Building on Vatican Leadership in AI Ethics

This summit is a direct continuation of the historic World Meeting on Human Fraternity held in Rome on September 12-13, 2025 (fraternitymeeting.org), where leading AI scholars and technologists signed an Appeal to "Pope Leo XIV and all global leaders and to all people of good will" for responsible AI development.

The Budapest Summit transforms the Vatican's moral leadership into practical action, building on the Human Fraternity Appeal to create concrete frameworks for digital & AI peace, sovereign AI governance, and the prohibition of autonomous weapons. Cardinal Silvano M. Tomasi will ensure continuity between the Rome Appeal and the Budapest Declaration, representing the Vatican's sustained commitment to human dignity in the digital age.

Digital Warfare Crisis

Cyber misinformation campaigns, attacks on critical civilian infrastructure, and weaponized AI systems have fundamentally transformed the nature of warfare. This conflict demonstrates the urgent need for international norms governing digital combat.

AI-Driven Autonomous Weapons

The deployment of AI-powered autonomous weapons systems in this conflict poses unprecedented risks to humanity. We must establish international agreements preventing the proliferation of killer robots and maintaining human control over life-and-death decisions.

Diplomatic Solution

Moving beyond condemnation to constructive high-level dialogue in the style of Munich Security Conference and Bilderberg meetings—emphasizing discretion, depth, and actionable outcomes for digital & AI peace.

Digital & AI Peace Summit Themes

01

Ceasing Digital Warfare

Addressing the immediate crisis in Ukraine: how to end cyber warfare in the current conflict and establish binding international norms to prevent future digital escalation in any theater of war.

  • Weaponized disinformation campaigns
  • Attacks on civilian digital infrastructure
  • International cyber warfare legislation
  • Digital Geneva Conventions for civilians
02

AI-Autonomous Weapons

Preventing the proliferation of AI-driven autonomous weapons systems and establishing global agreements for responsible AI in military applications.

  • Autonomous weapons systems
  • AI-powered surveillance
  • Military AI ethics
  • Human oversight requirements
03

Digital & AI Peace Framework

Building institutional mechanisms for digital & AI peace, including early warning systems, diplomatic protocols, and enforcement mechanisms for digital warfare prevention.

  • Digital peace treaties
  • Cyber diplomacy protocols
  • Early warning systems
  • International enforcement

Key Summit Initiatives

The Budapest Appeal

A formal declaration calling for the immediate cessation of digital warfare in the Ukraine-Russia conflict and the establishment of international norms preventing future cyber warfare escalation.

Tree of Peace Ceremonies

Two symbolic ceremonies unite tradition and technology: A drone light performance creates a luminous "Tree of Religions" over historic Heroes' Square adjacent to the Museum of Fine Arts, while a tree from the Vatican Gardens will be planted at Szabadság tér (Liberty Square).

Budapest Declaration

The Budapest Declaration on Digital & AI Peace & Sovereign AI will establish binding principles for responsible AI development and deployment in military and civilian contexts.

This summit follows the format of high-level diplomatic gatherings like the Munich Security Conference and Bilderberg meetings—emphasizing discretion, deep dialogue, and actionable outcomes with a 10 million euro budget for world-class production.

Summit Deliverables

A formal declaration calling for the immediate cessation of digital warfare in the Ukraine-Russia conflict and the establishment of binding international norms preventing future cyber warfare escalation.

Tree of Peace Ceremony

A symbolic tree from the Vatican Gardens will be planted at Szabadság tér (Liberty Square), representing our commitment to growing digital & AI peace from the seeds of dialogue and international cooperation.

Budapest Declaration
