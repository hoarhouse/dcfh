Summit Agenda

Two Days of Dialogue, Discovery, and Action

March 2-3, 2026

Summit Overview

The Global Digital Peace Summit brings together 100-300 carefully selected leaders over two intensive days. In the tradition of Munich Security Conference and Bilderberg meetings, each day builds toward concrete action for ending digital warfare.

Day 1: Inauguration, Budapest Appeal & Interfaith Digital Symbolism

Monday, March 2, 2026

Opening ceremony, presentation of the Budapest Appeal for Cyber Ceasefire, and interfaith digital symbolic gesture.

8:45 AM - 9:15 AM

Arrival & Reception

Registration

Welcome reception, registration, and networking at the Museum of Fine Arts.

9:15 AM - 9:35 AM

Opening Ceremony

Official Opening

Welcome address by Cardinal Péter Erdő, Archbishop of Esztergom-Budapest, and opening statements by summit organizers.

Cardinal Péter Erdő & Summit Leadership

9:35 AM - 10:10 AM

Foundational Addresses

Keynote Speeches

Visionary addresses on digital peace, AI ethics, and the urgent need for cyber ceasefire in current conflicts.

Cardinal Silvano M. Tomasi, Prof. Geoffrey Hinton (Nobel Laureate), Prof. Muhammad Yunus (Nobel Peace Prize)

10:10 AM - 10:45 AM

Presentation of the Budapest Appeal

Declaration

Official presentation of the Budapest Appeal for Cyber Ceasefire and Digital Peace, outlining concrete steps toward ending digital warfare.

10:45 AM - 11:05 AM

Break & Gallery Walk

Networking

Coffee break with opportunity to view the "Digital Peace Through Art" exhibition.

11:05 AM - 12:30 PM

Ministerial & Leaders' Roundtable

High-Level Discussion

Government ministers, tech leaders, and civil society representatives discuss implementation pathways for the Budapest Appeal.

Moderated by senior diplomatic leadership

12:30 PM - 2:00 PM

Diplomatic Luncheon

Networking

High-level diplomatic luncheon with structured networking opportunities.

2:30 PM - 3:30 PM

Participatory Session I: Understanding Digital Conflict

Interactive Workshop

Interactive sessions exploring the current state of digital warfare, cyber weapons, and their humanitarian impact.

3:30 PM - 4:30 PM

Participatory Session II: Pathways to Peace

Interactive Workshop

Collaborative exploration of concrete solutions for digital peace and cyber ceasefire implementation.

5:00 PM - 6:00 PM

Interfaith Digital Symbolic Gesture: Tree of Religions

Ceremony

A powerful digital art installation connecting Budapest to the Vatican Gardens, featuring synchronized drone displays forming a "Tree of Religions" as a symbol of unity for digital peace.

Heroes' Square

7:00 PM - 9:00 PM

Gala Evening: "Harmony for Peace"

Evening Event

Cultural celebration featuring performances by the Budapest Philharmonic Orchestra, interfaith choir, and traditional Hungarian artists. A evening dedicated to the harmony of cultures working together for digital peace.

Hungarian State Opera House

Day 2: Implementation, Governance & Digital Peace Commons

Tuesday, March 3, 2026

Working sessions on implementation strategies, governance frameworks, and the adoption of the Budapest Declaration for Digital Peace & Sovereign AI.

9:00 AM - 9:45 AM

High-Level Plenary: From Appeal to Action

Plenary Session

Senior leaders present implementation roadmaps for the Budapest Appeal, focusing on immediate actions and long-term strategies.

Government Ministers & Tech Industry Leaders

9:45 AM - 10:45 AM

Participatory Workshops: Four Parallel Tracks

Parallel Workshops

Four parallel tracks: Digital Ceasefire Mechanisms, AI Governance for Peace, Sovereign Technology Solutions, and Civil Society & Digital Rights.

10:45 AM - 11:00 AM

Break

Networking

11:00 AM - 12:15 PM

Consolidation Session: Building the Digital Peace Commons

Plenary

Synthesis of workshop outcomes and presentation of the Digital Peace Commons framework for ongoing collaboration.

12:15 PM - 1:15 PM

Working Lunch

Networking

1:15 PM - 2:30 PM

Leaders' Dialogue: Commitments for Digital Peace

High-Level Discussion

  • Working Group: Drafting AI Ethical Guidelines

Digital Sovereignty

Track 3

  • Working Group: Design Principles for Human-Centered Tech

  • Discussion: Faith Communities' Role in Digital Health
3:30 PM - 4:00 PM

Afternoon Break

Networking

4:00 PM - 5:30 PM

Track Reports & Cross-Pollination

Plenary Session

Representatives from each track present key insights and proposals. Open discussion on connections between tracks and opportunities for collaboration.

5:30 PM - 7:00 PM

Free Time

Break

Explore Budapest, rest, or continue informal conversations.

Evening Reflection & Networking Dinner

Evening Event

Casual dinner with optional faith reflection led by summit chaplain. Time for deeper conversation and relationship building.
