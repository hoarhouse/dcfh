Featured Speakers

Cardinal Silvano M. Tomasi

Vatican Diplomat & Technology Ethics Leader

Former Permanent Observer of the Holy See to the United Nations and leading voice on technology ethics within the Vatican. Key architect of the Vatican's AI ethics initiatives and international diplomatic efforts on digital peace.

Session: "Vatican Diplomacy and Digital Peace"

Prof. Muhammad Yunus

Nobel Peace Prize Laureate & Social Business Pioneer

2006 Nobel Peace Prize winner for pioneering microcredit and social business concepts. Leading advocate for technology serving the poor and marginalized, with extensive work on digital inclusion and peaceful technology deployment.

Session: "Technology for Social Peace and Human Development"

Prof. Michael Andersson

Director, EU Digital Policy Institute

Former EU Commissioner advisor and leading expert on European digital sovereignty. Specializes in international technology governance and digital rights frameworks across borders.

Founder, African Digital Rights Initiative

Pioneering advocate for digital inclusion and rights across the Global South. Former UN technology advisor with expertise in bridging digital divides and ensuring equitable access to emerging technologies.
