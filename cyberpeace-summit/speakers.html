Distinguished Speakers

Visionary leaders shaping the future of digital & AI peace

6 March 2026 • Budapest, Hungary

Keynote Address

Cardinal Silvano M. Tomasi

Former Vatican Representative to the United Nations • Vatican AI Ethics Expert

Cardinal Silvano M. Tomasi is a key Vatican voice on AI ethics and technology governance. As the former Vatican representative to the United Nations in Geneva, he has extensive experience in international diplomacy and human rights. Cardinal Tomasi has been instrumental in developing the Vatican's approach to artificial intelligence ethics and continues to advocate for human dignity in the digital age through his work with the Vatican's technology initiatives. His decades of diplomatic service and deep theological insight position him uniquely to bridge the conversation between technological advancement and timeless spiritual values, ensuring that artificial intelligence development remains centered on human flourishing and the common good.

Keynote Topic

"Faith, Technology, and Human Flourishing in the Digital Age"

Opening the summit with profound reflections on how traditional wisdom and eternal truths can guide our digital future while preserving human dignity and promoting authentic peace. Cardinal Tomasi will explore the intersection of faith and artificial intelligence, offering a moral framework for technological development that honors the sacred nature of human consciousness and creativity.

Featured Speakers

Distinguished leaders from technology, policy, and international organizations bringing diverse perspectives to advance digital & AI peace.

Cardinal Silvano M. Tomasi

Vatican Diplomat & Technology Ethics Leader

Former Permanent Observer of the Holy See to the United Nations and leading voice on technology ethics within the Vatican. Key architect of the Vatican's AI ethics initiatives and international diplomatic efforts on digital & AI peace. His extensive experience in international diplomacy and human rights positions him as a crucial bridge between traditional wisdom and emerging technologies.

Session

Vatican Diplomacy and Digital & AI Peace

Prof. Geoffrey Hinton

Nobel Laureate • "Godfather of AI" • AI Safety Advocate

Co-recipient of the 2024 Nobel Prize in Physics for foundational discoveries in artificial neural networks and deep learning. Widely regarded as the "Godfather of AI," Prof. Hinton's pioneering work on backpropagation and neural networks laid the foundation for modern artificial intelligence. After decades at Google Brain, he now focuses exclusively on AI safety and ethics, warning of existential risks while advocating for responsible AI development. His moral leadership on AI alignment and safety has reshaped global discourse on technology's future.

Session

AI Safety, Ethics, and the Path to Digital & AI Peace

Prof. Muhammad Yunus

Nobel Peace Laureate • Grameen Bank Founder

Awarded the 2006 Nobel Peace Prize for pioneering microcredit and microfinance, Prof. Yunus revolutionized economic development through his concept of "Social Business." Founder of Grameen Bank and over 50 other companies dedicated to solving social problems, he is a global leader advocating for technology serving humanity's poorest and most marginalized. His vision of peaceful technology deployment and digital inclusion has influenced policy worldwide.

Session

Technology for Social Peace and Human Development

Swami Sarvapriyananda

Spiritual Leader • Vedanta Scholar

Renowned interfaith voice and scholar of Advaita Vedanta philosophy, Swami Sarvapriyananda is a leading advocate for harmony between science and spirituality. As Minister and Spiritual Leader of the Vedanta Society of New York, he brings profound insights from Eastern wisdom traditions to contemporary discussions on consciousness, artificial intelligence, and the nature of human experience. His teachings bridge ancient philosophical wisdom with modern technological challenges, offering unique perspectives on digital ethics and human flourishing.

Session

Eastern Wisdom and Digital Consciousness: Vedantic Perspectives on AI

Prof. Michael Andersson

Director, EU Digital Policy Institute

Former EU Commissioner advisor and leading expert on European digital sovereignty. Specializes in international technology governance and digital rights frameworks across borders. His work focuses on balancing technological innovation with democratic values and fundamental rights. He has been instrumental in shaping EU's approach to AI regulation and data sovereignty.

Session

Digital Sovereignty and International Cooperation

Dr. Amara Okafor

Founder, African Digital Rights Initiative

Pioneering advocate for digital inclusion and rights across the Global South. Former UN technology advisor with expertise in bridging digital divides and ensuring equitable access to emerging technologies. Dr. Okafor has led groundbreaking initiatives to bring internet access and digital literacy to underserved communities across Africa, championing the principle that digital rights are fundamental human rights.

Session

Digital & AI Peace Through Global Inclusion

Ambassador Lisa Rodriguez

UN Special Rapporteur on Digital Rights

Diplomatic leader in international technology policy and human rights. Has negotiated key UN resolutions on digital governance and leads efforts to establish global norms for peaceful technology use. Ambassador Rodriguez's work has been instrumental in creating the legal frameworks necessary for international cooperation on cyber conflict prevention and digital rights protection.

Session

International Law in the Digital Age

Dr. Raj Patel

Chief Technology Officer, Peace Tech Alliance

Innovative technologist focused on conflict prevention and digital & AI peace technologies. Leads development of AI systems designed to detect and mitigate digital warfare and cyber conflict escalation. Dr. Patel's pioneering work in predictive peace technology has helped prevent numerous cyber conflicts through early warning systems and diplomatic intervention tools.

Session

AI for Cyber Conflict Prevention

40 Carefully Selected Participants

This invitation-only summit follows the format of Munich Security Conference and Bilderberg meetings, bringing together global leaders in technology, diplomacy, and civil society for deep dialogue on digital warfare and cyber peace.

Additional participants include tech executives working on AI weapons systems, diplomatic leaders from conflict zones, UN peacekeeping technology experts, and researchers specializing in cyber warfare prevention and digital & AI peace frameworks.
