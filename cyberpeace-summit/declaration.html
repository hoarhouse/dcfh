Summit Declarations & Outcomes

Historic commitments for digital peace and human dignity

March 2-3, 2026 • Budapest, Hungary

Four Historic Deliverables

The Budapest Digital Peace Summit will produce four landmark documents that establish the foundation for global digital peace, ethical AI governance, and the protection of human dignity in cyberspace. These deliverables represent unprecedented cooperation between technology leaders, faith communities, governments, and civil society.

1

Budapest Appeal for Cyber Ceasefire and Digital Peace

An urgent diplomatic appeal calling for the immediate cessation of digital warfare in the Russia-Ukraine conflict and establishing precedent for future cyber ceasefires worldwide.

Purpose and Scope

The Budapest Appeal represents the first formal international call for a comprehensive cyber ceasefire in an active conflict zone. This unprecedented document addresses the humanitarian crisis created by digital warfare targeting civilian infrastructure, healthcare systems, and essential services.

Key Commitments

Immediate Cessation

Call for all parties to immediately halt cyber attacks on civilian infrastructure, including power grids, water systems, healthcare facilities, and educational institutions.

Humanitarian Corridors

Establishment of protected digital humanitarian corridors ensuring uninterrupted access to essential online services, medical systems, and emergency communications.

Verification Mechanisms

Creation of independent international monitoring bodies to verify compliance with cyber ceasefire commitments and investigate violations of digital peace agreements.

Addressing the Russia-Ukraine Digital Conflict

The Appeal specifically addresses the unprecedented scale of cyber warfare in the Russia-Ukraine conflict, including:

  • Prohibition of attacks on civilian digital infrastructure
  • Protection of healthcare and educational systems from cyber assault
  • Prevention of weaponized disinformation targeting civilian populations
  • Safeguarding of essential services and critical infrastructure
  • Establishment of neutral digital zones for humanitarian operations
2

Budapest Declaration for Digital Peace & Sovereign AI

A comprehensive framework establishing global principles for ethical AI governance, digital sovereignty, and the protection of human dignity in the age of artificial intelligence.

Declaration Goals and Principles

The Budapest Declaration establishes foundational principles for ensuring that artificial intelligence serves humanity rather than threatening it. Building on the Vatican's Rome Call for AI Ethics and international human rights frameworks, this declaration creates binding commitments for ethical AI development.

Core Principles

Human Dignity

All AI systems must respect and protect fundamental human dignity, ensuring technology serves humanity rather than diminishing or replacing human agency.

Transparency & Accountability

AI decision-making processes must be explainable, auditable, and subject to human oversight, with clear chains of responsibility for AI-driven outcomes.

Justice & Non-Discrimination

AI systems must promote equity and fairness, actively preventing algorithmic bias and ensuring equal access to AI benefits across all communities.

Sovereign AI and Data Sovereignty

The Declaration establishes crucial frameworks for national digital sovereignty:

  • Right of nations to govern AI development within their borders
  • Protection of citizen data from unauthorized foreign surveillance
  • Democratic control over AI systems affecting national security
  • Prohibition of AI-driven foreign interference in domestic affairs
  • Support for developing nations in building sovereign AI capabilities

Connection to Ethical AI Governance

The Declaration mandates the establishment of national and international AI governance bodies that ensure all artificial intelligence development adheres to ethical principles, with particular emphasis on preventing autonomous weapons systems and protecting vulnerable populations from AI-driven harm.

3

Budapest Action Framework for Digital Peace

A practical roadmap for implementing digital peace initiatives, with concrete timelines, measurable commitments, and governance mechanisms for 2026-2027.

Implementation Steps

The Action Framework translates summit principles into concrete actions through a phased approach:

Phase 1: Immediate Actions (March-June 2026)

  • Establishment of International Digital Peace Commission
  • Launch of emergency cyber ceasefire monitoring in conflict zones
  • Creation of rapid response teams for digital humanitarian crises
  • Implementation of AI weapons system moratorium

Phase 2: Institution Building (July-December 2026)

  • Founding of Budapest Institute for Digital Peace Studies
  • Development of international cyber conflict mediation protocols
  • Establishment of sovereign AI certification programs
  • Launch of digital peace education initiatives

Phase 3: Global Expansion (2027)

  • Regional digital peace summits on six continents
  • Integration with UN peacekeeping operations
  • Global treaty on autonomous weapons prohibition
  • Universal digital rights charter adoption

Governance Mechanisms

Executive Council

15-member body including representatives from technology, faith communities, governments, and civil society to oversee implementation of summit commitments.

Technical Working Groups

Specialized teams addressing specific challenges including AI safety, cyber conflict prevention, digital rights protection, and sovereign technology development.

Compliance Monitoring

Independent verification bodies ensuring signatories fulfill their commitments, with transparent reporting mechanisms and accountability measures.

4

"Integral Ecology for Digital Peace" Conceptual Map

A visionary framework connecting environmental sustainability, digital technology, and human flourishing, grounded in Pope Francis's Laudato Si' and the principle of Integral Human Development.

Foundation in Laudato Si' and Integral Human Development

Drawing from Pope Francis's encyclical on care for our common home, this conceptual map recognizes that digital peace cannot be separated from environmental justice, social equity, and spiritual well-being. Just as Laudato Si' calls for an "integral ecology" addressing all aspects of the environmental crisis, we propose an integral approach to digital peace that considers technology's impact on all dimensions of human and planetary life.

Cross-Religious Perspectives

Catholic Social Teaching

Technology must serve the common good, protect human dignity, and promote solidarity among all peoples, with preferential option for the poor and vulnerable.

Eastern Wisdom Traditions

Buddhist and Hindu perspectives on consciousness, interconnectedness, and non-violence inform our understanding of AI consciousness and digital karma.

Abrahamic Dialogue

Jewish, Christian, and Islamic teachings on stewardship, justice, and peace converge to guide ethical technology development serving all humanity.

Cross-Scientific Approaches

The conceptual map integrates insights from multiple scientific disciplines:

  • Computer Science: Technical frameworks for secure, transparent, and ethical AI systems
  • Neuroscience: Understanding consciousness and human-AI interaction
  • Environmental Science: Assessing technology's ecological footprint and sustainability
  • Social Sciences: Analyzing digital technology's impact on communities and cultures
  • Peace Studies: Applying conflict resolution principles to cyber warfare

Value-Based Solutions for Cyber Peace

Human Dignity as Foundation

Every technological decision must be evaluated through its impact on human dignity, ensuring technology enhances rather than diminishes our humanity.

Common Good Over Profit

Digital infrastructure and AI capabilities must serve collective human flourishing rather than narrow commercial or military interests.

Intergenerational Justice

Decisions about AI and digital systems must consider their impact on future generations, ensuring we leave a peaceful and sustainable digital world.

Subsidiarity and Participation

Digital governance must respect local autonomy while ensuring global coordination, with meaningful participation from all affected communities.

Implementation Through Integral Approach

The Integral Ecology for Digital Peace provides a holistic framework that:

  • Connects digital peace with environmental sustainability
  • Integrates spiritual wisdom with technological innovation
  • Bridges scientific knowledge with ethical values
  • Unites diverse faith traditions in common purpose
  • Transforms technology from a tool of division to an instrument of unity
