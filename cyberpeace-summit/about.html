About the Summit

Building Bridges Between Faith, Technology, and Human Dignity

Our Purpose

War has traditionally been fought on physical battlegrounds with soldiers, mechanized equipment and armament. The dangers of technology entered the battlefield in World War II with the use of nuclear weapons and the beginning of a nuclear arms race. In the mid-2010s, a military artificial intelligence arms race began to emerge with advance AI technologies and lethal autonomous weaponry systems. Digital & AI weaponization has expanded today to include cyber misinformation, cyber disruption of fundamental human services, cyber weapons, unmanned drones, all of which risks are escalated with autonomous and AI driven systems ceding decisions to machines and future artificial superintelligence. With these rapidly advancing digital & AI technologies, a peace treaty to end a military conflict alone is no longer enough. To attain complete and sustainable integral peace, solutions in both the physical and digital & AI dimensions are required.

DCF is a constellation of non-profits dedicated to advancing human-centric, ethical, value-based, safe Sovereign AI, Data Sovereignty, and emerging technologies for peace and the common good, and is honored to convene leaders from throughout the globe in technology, faith, government, and academia in this Digital Peace & AI Summit in Budapest, Hungary for the purpose and goal of working together in a commons for the path to prevent and cease the use of digital and AI autonomous weapons, artificial superintelligence in weapons, and cyber warfare.

Summit Goals

  • Focus: Bring together leaders in technology, faith, government and academia
  • Purpose: Integrate values in development of sovereign AI, data sovereignty and value-based AI for Integral Digital & AI Peace
  • Goal: Finalize and produce a Declaration for Digital Peace & Sovereign AI for a Cyber Ceasefire and Digital & AI Peace

Building on Vatican Leadership

The Budapest Global Summit is a direct follow-on from the historic World Meeting on Human Fraternity held in Rome on September 12-13, 2025, bringing Vatican dialogue on AI ethics into practical implementation.

From Rome to Budapest: A Continuum of Action

The September 2025 World Meeting on Human Fraternity in Rome (fraternitymeeting.org) brought together leading scholars, technologists, and faith leaders to address the urgent ethical challenges posed by artificial intelligence. This extraordinary gathering produced a signed Appeal from the world's foremost AI researchers and technologists addressed to "Pope Leo XIV and all global leaders and to all people of good will" calling for responsible AI development that serves human dignity.

The Budapest Summit builds directly on this foundation, transforming the moral imperatives articulated in Rome into practical implementation frameworks. While the Rome meeting established the ethical principles, Budapest will create the concrete mechanisms for digital & AI peace, sovereign AI governance, and the prohibition of autonomous weapons systems.

Continuity of Vatican Engagement

This progression from Rome to Budapest represents the Vatican's sustained commitment to ensuring technology serves humanity. Cardinal Silvano M. Tomasi, a key architect of the Vatican's AI ethics initiatives who played a central role in the Rome meeting, will help bridge these efforts at the Budapest Summit, ensuring continuity between the Human Fraternity Appeal and the Budapest Declaration for Digital & AI Peace.

Why Now?

We stand at a pivotal moment in human history. Artificial intelligence is reshaping every aspect of society—from how we work and learn to how we relate to one another and understand ourselves.

Digital Warfare Crisis

The Russia-Ukraine conflict has demonstrated how cyber weapons, AI-driven disinformation, and attacks on digital infrastructure have transformed warfare. For the first time in history, we are witnessing a war fought simultaneously in both physical and digital dimensions at scale.

AI-Powered Autonomous Weapons

The deployment of artificial intelligence in military systems raises unprecedented ethical concerns. Autonomous weapons that can select and engage targets without human intervention threaten to fundamentally alter the nature of armed conflict and challenge basic principles of human dignity.

Civilian Impact

Digital warfare targets civilian infrastructure—power grids, hospitals, schools. Weaponized disinformation campaigns undermine democratic discourse and social cohesion. The distinction between combatants and civilians becomes blurred in cyberspace.

The Peace Imperative

This crisis demands immediate action to establish international norms for digital & AI peace. We must cease current digital warfare and prevent future escalation through diplomatic dialogue, binding agreements, and technological frameworks that serve human dignity rather than destruction.

Why Budapest?

Budapest is uniquely positioned to host this historic conversation. As a city that bridges East and West, with deep Catholic heritage and a thriving tech ecosystem, Budapest embodies the synthesis we seek.

Bridge Between Worlds

Geographically and culturally, Budapest connects Eastern and Western Europe, serving as a natural meeting place for diverse perspectives on technology, governance, and human values.

Catholic Heritage

With strong Vatican representation through Cardinal Silvano M. Tomasi, the summit offers deep engagement with Catholic social teaching on technology and human dignity, building on Vatican AI ethics initiatives.

Emerging Tech Hub

Budapest's growing technology sector demonstrates that innovation and ethical reflection can coexist. The city is home to startups, research institutions, and tech companies building the future responsibly.

Historical Perspective

A city that has witnessed both the perils of totalitarian surveillance and the triumph of human dignity, Budapest brings historical wisdom to contemporary debates about power, privacy, and freedom.

About the Organizer

Domus Communis Foundation

The Domus Communis Foundation is a constellation of non-profit organizations dedicated to human-centric, ethical, value-based, safe, Sovereign AI and Data Sovereignty. Our mission is advancing digital & AI peace and the common good through technology that serves human dignity rather than enabling warfare and surveillance.

Our Focus Areas

Digital & AI Peace Research

Developing frameworks for ending digital warfare and preventing cyber conflict escalation, grounded in diplomatic principles and universal human rights.

Sovereign AI Development

Advocating for AI systems that serve national sovereignty and democratic values rather than enabling surveillance and autonomous weapons.

Faith-Tech Dialogue

Creating spaces where religious leaders, ethicists, and technologists can engage in meaningful conversation about our digital future.

Global Network Building

Connecting Catholic institutions, tech leaders, and civil society organizations working toward ethical technology worldwide.

Our Vision for the Summit

The Budapest Global Summit represents the culmination of our work to date and a launchpad for the future. We envision:

  • Immediate cessation of digital warfare in the Ukraine-Russia conflict
  • Binding international agreements preventing future cyber warfare escalation
  • Diplomatic frameworks for digital & AI peace and conflict prevention
  • International norms prohibiting AI-driven autonomous weapons systems
  • Budapest Declaration establishing comprehensive digital & AI peace principles
