Nuclear Disarmament & AI Governance

Preventing the integration of artificial intelligence into nuclear weapons systems and establishing transparent, accountable governance frameworks for military AI technologies worldwide.

Our Comprehensive Approach

DCF's Nuclear Disarmament Initiative combines diplomatic advocacy, technical research, and ethical framework development. We work directly with nuclear powers, UN agencies, and civil society organizations to establish binding international agreements that prevent AI-nuclear integration while advancing broader nuclear disarmament goals.

Grounded in the Vatican's teaching that nuclear weapons are immoral, our initiative represents the most comprehensive faith-based approach to nuclear disarmament in the AI era, bringing together Catholic institutions worldwide in service of global peace and security.

Initiative Scope

  • UN Working Group leadership and policy development
  • Nuclear-AI risk assessment and modeling
  • International treaty negotiation support
  • Military AI transparency standards development
  • Faith-based advocacy and education campaigns
  • Technical verification systems design
  • Nuclear doctrine modernization guidance
  • Civil society coalition building

Our Progress Toward Nuclear Peace

Building momentum for binding international agreements that prevent AI-nuclear integration while advancing comprehensive nuclear disarmament.

Foundations Established

Successfully launched UN Working Group participation, drafted 23 treaty articles, engaged 45 countries including 7 nuclear powers, and established the Vatican Nuclear Commission with Cardinal Turkson as chair.

Current Negotiations

Leading treaty negotiations on AI-nuclear non-integration, developing verification protocols with IAEA partnership, and building global Catholic coalition with 200+ bishops supporting nuclear abolition.

2025-2026 Goals

Finalize binding AI-Nuclear Non-Integration Treaty, establish International AI-Nuclear Monitoring Agency, achieve formal Vatican endorsement, and secure commitments from remaining nuclear powers.
