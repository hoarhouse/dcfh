Why Budapest?

Central European Location

Budapest sits at the heart of Europe, making it easily accessible from both Western and Eastern regions. As a crossroads of cultures and civilizations, it symbolically represents the summit's mission to bridge divides and build consensus across different perspectives.

The city's excellent transportation infrastructure, including direct flights from major global hubs and high-speed rail connections throughout Europe, ensures participants from around the world can easily attend.

Historical Significance

Budapest's history as a city that has witnessed both the devastation of war and the triumph of peace makes it a powerful location for discussions on preventing digital conflict. The city's transformation from Cold War division to European integration parallels our hope for digital peace.

Hungary's strong Catholic heritage and active faith community provide a supportive environment for a summit grounded in Catholic social teaching while welcoming diverse perspectives.

Travel Accessibility

Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport serves over 150 destinations worldwide with numerous daily connections to major cities. The city is also a major railway hub with direct trains from Vienna, Prague, Berlin, and other European capitals.

Within the city, an efficient public transportation system including metro, tram, and bus networks makes it easy to navigate between the venue, hotels, and cultural sites.

Cultural Appeal

Budapest offers participants a rich cultural experience beyond the summit sessions. The city's stunning architecture, from Gothic churches to Art Nouveau masterpieces, provides an inspiring backdrop for contemplating humanity's creative potential.

The famous Hungarian hospitality, vibrant cultural scene, and excellent cuisine ensure that participants will have memorable experiences that foster the informal connections often as valuable as formal sessions.

Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport (BUD)

  • Located 16 km southeast of the city center
  • Direct flights from major European cities (1-3 hours)
  • Connections from North America, Asia, and Middle East via major hubs
  • Airport shuttle bus (100E) provides direct connection to city center in 30-40 minutes
  • Taxi and ride-sharing services available (approximately 25-30 minute journey)

=‚ By Train

International Rail Connections

  • Budapest Keleti and Nyugati are the main international railway stations
  • Direct trains from Vienna (2.5 hours), Prague (7 hours), Berlin (11 hours)
  • Night trains available from Munich, Zurich, and other major cities
  • All main stations connected to metro system for easy city access
  • Consider scenic Danube route from Vienna combining train and river transport

Discover Budapest

Take advantage of your visit to explore one of Europe's most beautiful capitals. Budapest offers unforgettable experiences for every interest.

Historic & Cultural Sites

Buda Castle & Castle District

UNESCO World Heritage site with stunning views over the Danube, housing the Hungarian National Gallery and Budapest History Museum.

Parliament Building

One of Europe's most beautiful parliament buildings, offering guided tours of its magnificent interior including the Hungarian Crown Jewels.

St. Stephen's Basilica

Hungary's most important church, featuring beautiful architecture and panoramic views from its dome. Regular organ concerts provide spiritual moments.

Heroes' Square & City Park

Iconic square commemorating Hungarian history, adjacent to City Park with Széchenyi Thermal Baths and Vajdahunyad Castle.

Experiences & Activities

Danube River Cruises

Evening cruises offer spectacular views of illuminated bridges and riverside monuments, perfect for networking in a relaxed setting.

Thermal Baths

Experience Budapest's famous spa culture at historic baths like Gellért or Széchenyi, perfect for relaxation after summit sessions.

Central Market Hall

Historic market offering local products, Hungarian crafts, and traditional cuisine. Ideal for finding authentic souvenirs.

Ruin Bars

Budapest's unique bar culture in transformed abandoned buildings offers informal networking opportunities in creative atmospheres.
