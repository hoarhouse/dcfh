Uniting Faith and Technology for Global Peace

A critical moment for humanity's digital future

Why This Matters

The rapid advancement of artificial intelligence in weapons systems and the increasing vulnerability of nuclear command systems to cyber attacks represent existential threats to humanity. As Catholic leaders and technologists, we have a moral imperative to address these challenges before they become irreversible. This summit brings together the wisdom of faith tradition with cutting-edge technological expertise to forge a path toward digital peace.

Our Unique Approach

Unlike purely technical conferences or traditional religious gatherings, the Cyberpeace Summit integrates Catholic social teaching with practical policy solutions. We bridge the gap between moral philosophy and technical implementation, creating actionable frameworks that respect human dignity while addressing real-world security concerns. Our multi-stakeholder approach ensures that solutions are both ethically grounded and technically feasible.

Expected Impact

This summit will produce concrete policy recommendations for national governments and international bodies, establish a permanent Catholic working group on cyber weapons ethics, and launch a global interfaith initiative on AI governance. Participants will leave with practical tools for implementing cyberpeace principles in their organizations and communities, contributing to a coordinated global response to these critical challenges.

Key Summit Themes

Critical issues at the intersection of faith, technology, and peace

Autonomous Weapons

Examining the moral implications of AI systems that can select and engage targets without human intervention. We'll explore Catholic perspectives on preserving human agency in life-and-death decisions and developing ethical frameworks for military AI.

Nuclear Command Vulnerabilities

Addressing the growing risk of cyber attacks on nuclear command, control, and communications systems. The summit will assess current vulnerabilities and propose faith-informed approaches to reducing the risk of accidental nuclear war.

AI-Driven Conflict

Understanding how artificial intelligence accelerates the pace of warfare and decision-making beyond human comprehension. We'll discuss the theological and practical challenges of maintaining human dignity in an age of algorithmic warfare.

Digital Peace Frameworks

Developing comprehensive approaches to digital peacebuilding that integrate Catholic social teaching with international law. The summit will work toward actionable frameworks for responsible state behavior in cyberspace.

Why February 2026 Matters

A critical window for global action

Policy Windows Closing

The international community is at a crossroads. Key decisions about AI governance and cyber weapons are being made now that will shape the next decades. The UN's discussions on autonomous weapons systems are reaching critical junctures, and national AI strategies are being finalized. February 2026 represents a unique opportunity to influence these processes before positions become entrenched.

Major powers are rapidly developing and deploying AI-enabled weapons systems. Without immediate intervention from moral authorities and civil society, we risk normalizing technologies that fundamentally challenge human dignity and the principles of just war theory.

Accelerating Technological Change

The pace of AI development is exponential, not linear. Systems being designed today will be deployed at scale by 2027. The decisions we make in 2026 about ethical boundaries and regulatory frameworks will determine whether these technologies serve peace or accelerate conflict.

Recent incidents of AI hallucinations in military contexts and near-misses in nuclear command systems underscore the urgency. We cannot wait for a catastrophe to act. The Catholic Church's moral authority and global reach position it uniquely to convene stakeholders and drive consensus before it's too late.
