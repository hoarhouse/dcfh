About the Summit

The Cyberpeace Summit 2026 represents a historic convergence of faith leaders, technologists, and policymakers addressing the existential challenges posed by weaponized artificial intelligence and cyber threats to global stability.

The Challenge We Face

Autonomous Weapons Development

Military powers worldwide are racing to develop lethal autonomous weapons systems (LAWS) that can select and engage targets without meaningful human control. These systems fundamentally challenge our understanding of accountability, proportionality, and discrimination in warfare. Current development trajectories suggest that fully autonomous weapons could be deployed within the next five years, creating a new arms race that threatens global stability.

The speed of AI decision-making exceeds human cognitive capabilities, potentially leading to escalation spirals that humans cannot interrupt. Without immediate intervention, we risk creating weapons that operate beyond human moral judgment, undermining centuries of just war tradition and international humanitarian law.

Nuclear Command System Vulnerabilities

Nuclear command, control, and communications (NC3) systems increasingly rely on digital infrastructure vulnerable to cyber attacks. Recent assessments reveal that even minor intrusions could trigger false warnings or compromise decision-making processes during crises. The integration of AI into early warning systems introduces new risks of misinterpretation and automated escalation.

The compressed decision timelines in nuclear scenarios, combined with AI-driven analysis, create unprecedented risks of accidental war. A single algorithmic error or successful cyber intrusion could initiate an irreversible chain of events, potentially leading to nuclear exchange based on false information.

AI Escalation Risks

Artificial intelligence systems operate at speeds that compress decision-making from hours to milliseconds. In conflict scenarios, AI-enabled systems could escalate confrontations faster than human leaders can assess situations or negotiate solutions. The interaction between multiple AI systems from different nations creates unpredictable dynamics that no single actor can control.

Machine learning algorithms trained on historical conflict data may perpetuate or amplify past patterns of violence, while lacking the human capacity for empathy, creativity in problem-solving, or recognition of unprecedented situations requiring restraint.

Need for Ethical Frameworks

Current international law and ethical frameworks were developed for human-controlled weapons and cannot adequately address the challenges of autonomous systems. Technical communities often lack grounding in moral philosophy, while ethicists may not fully grasp the technical realities of AI systems. This gap threatens to leave humanity without adequate guardrails as these technologies proliferate.

Religious traditions offer millennia of wisdom about violence, peace, and human dignity, but this wisdom must be translated into actionable principles for the digital age. The Catholic Church's global reach and moral authority position it uniquely to bridge these divides.

Our Approach

Catholic Social Teaching Meets Technology

The summit grounds technological discussions in two millennia of Catholic moral tradition. We draw upon principles of human dignity, the common good, subsidiarity, and solidarity to evaluate emerging technologies. This isn't about imposing religious doctrine on secular systems, but rather offering time-tested wisdom about human nature and social organization that transcends technological change.

Catholic social teaching provides a framework for evaluating not just what we can build, but what we should build. The principle of human dignity demands that lethal decisions remain under meaningful human control. The common good requires us to consider global impacts, not just national advantages. Subsidiarity guides us toward governance structures that respect both local contexts and universal principles.

Faith

Grounding technological progress in transcendent values and moral wisdom that has guided humanity through previous existential challenges.

Policy

Translating ethical principles into concrete regulatory frameworks and international agreements that can be implemented by governments and organizations.

Innovation

Engaging technologists to develop solutions that enhance rather than diminish human agency, creating technology that serves peace rather than conflict.

Global Coalition Building

The summit serves as a catalyst for unprecedented cooperation between religious institutions, civil society organizations, academic institutions, and technology companies. We're building bridges between communities that rarely interact directly: Vatican officials and Silicon Valley engineers, military ethicists and peace activists, Global South voices and Northern technology centers.

Our coalition-building approach recognizes that no single actor can address these challenges alone. By creating spaces for dialogue across traditional divides, we're fostering the relationships and mutual understanding necessary for coordinated global action. The summit isn't just an event but the beginning of an ongoing collaborative process.

Summit Format

Keynote Speeches

World-renowned experts will deliver addresses on critical topics including the theology of technology, military AI development, nuclear command vulnerabilities, and paths to digital peace. Each keynote will bridge theoretical understanding with practical applications, ensuring participants gain both conceptual clarity and actionable insights.

Speakers will include Vatican officials, leading AI researchers, military ethicists, peace advocates, and policy makers from multiple continents, ensuring diverse perspectives on these global challenges.

Working Groups

Specialized working groups will dive deep into specific challenges, developing concrete recommendations for different aspects of cyberpeace. Groups will focus on autonomous weapons governance, nuclear cyber security, AI ethics frameworks, and implementation strategies for different contexts.

Each working group will include diverse stakeholders to ensure recommendations are both principled and practical, with facilitated discussions designed to build consensus while respecting different perspectives.

Policy Workshops

Interactive workshops will translate ethical principles into policy language, training participants to engage effectively with government officials and international bodies. Participants will learn how to craft policy proposals, build coalitions, and navigate the complex landscape of technology governance.

Workshops will include case studies, role-playing exercises, and practical tools that participants can immediately apply in their own contexts, whether in government, civil society, or private sector roles.

Vatican Briefing

A special session will prepare comprehensive briefing materials for Vatican leadership, synthesizing summit discussions into actionable recommendations for Church engagement with these issues. This briefing will inform potential papal statements, Vatican diplomatic initiatives, and Catholic institutional responses worldwide.

The briefing will also establish ongoing communication channels between the Vatican and technology communities, ensuring continued dialogue beyond the summit itself.

Expected Outcomes

White Paper on Cyberpeace Principles

A comprehensive document outlining Catholic-informed principles for digital peace, including specific guidelines for autonomous weapons, nuclear cyber security, and AI governance. This white paper will serve as a reference for policymakers, technologists, and religious leaders worldwide, providing clear ethical frameworks for navigating complex technological challenges.

Policy Recommendations

Detailed policy proposals for national governments, the United Nations, regional bodies, and international organizations. These recommendations will include model legislation, treaty language, and implementation roadmaps that can be adapted to different political and cultural contexts while maintaining core ethical principles.

Global Coalition Formation

Establishment of an ongoing international coalition committed to implementing cyberpeace principles. This coalition will include religious institutions, civil society organizations, academic centers, and committed individuals who will continue working together long after the summit concludes, with regular meetings and coordinated campaigns.

Media Coverage Plan

Strategic communication initiatives to ensure summit outcomes reach global audiences, including op-eds in major publications, documentary partnerships, and social media campaigns. The plan will translate complex technical and ethical issues into accessible narratives that engage diverse audiences and build public support for cyberpeace initiatives.

About DCF Hungary

Digital Christian Fellowship Hungary is a pioneering organization at the intersection of faith and technology. Founded to explore how digital innovation can serve the common good while respecting human dignity, DCF Hungary has become a leading voice in Catholic technology ethics. Our initiatives include educational programs for church leaders on digital transformation, hackathons for social good, and regular dialogues between technologists and theologians.

Through partnerships with Vatican institutions, European technology centers, and global peace organizations, DCF Hungary has developed unique expertise in translating between technical and theological languages. Our work has influenced church positions on artificial intelligence, supported digital evangelization efforts, and fostered a new generation of Catholic technologists committed to building technology that serves humanity.
