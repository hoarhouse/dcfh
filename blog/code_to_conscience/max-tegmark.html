Max Tegmark is a physicist at MIT and a leading advocate for ensuring that artificial intelligence develops safely and in line with human values. Known for his ability to connect complex technical issues to the broader public, Tegmark co-founded the Future of Life Institute (FLI), a nonprofit dedicated to reducing existential risks from advanced technologies such as AI, biotechnology, and nuclear weapons.


Tegmark began his career in cosmology and physics, publishing widely on the structure of the universe. But as AI progressed rapidly in the 2010s, he shifted his focus toward what he views as one of the defining challenges of our century: aligning artificial intelligence with human survival and flourishing. He has argued that AI, if left unchecked, could magnify inequality, destabilize societies, or even surpass human control. At the same time, he sees AI as a powerful tool for solving pressing challenges‚Äîfrom curing diseases to addressing climate change‚Äîif it is developed responsibly.


Through FLI, Tegmark has played a central role in shaping global debates on AI. His institute coordinated the landmark 2015 open letter calling for robust AI safety research, signed by hundreds of top scientists and tech leaders including Stephen Hawking and Elon Musk. In 2017, FLI issued the Asilomar AI Principles, which became a reference point for ethical AI discussions worldwide.


Tegmark is also the author of the bestselling book Life 3.0: Being Human in the Age of Artificial Intelligence. In it, he explores scenarios for AI‚Äôs future, from utopian to dystopian, and stresses that humanity still has agency in determining the path. His blend of scientific expertise, activism, and public communication makes him one of the most influential voices ensuring that AI serves humanity rather than endangers it.
