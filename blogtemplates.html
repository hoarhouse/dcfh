AI Ethics in Catholic Education: Building a Framework for Tomorrow

By Dr. Michael Chen March 15, 2024 5 min read

As artificial intelligence becomes increasingly integrated into educational settings, Catholic institutions face unique opportunities and challenges in maintaining their values while embracing technological advancement. This intersection of faith and technology requires careful consideration and ethical frameworks that honor both innovation and tradition.

The Current Landscape

Catholic schools worldwide are beginning to adopt AI-powered tools for personalized learning, administrative efficiency, and student support. However, the implementation of these technologies must be guided by principles that reflect Catholic social teaching, particularly regarding human dignity, solidarity, and the common good.

"Technology is a wonderful fruit of human creativity, but it must always serve the human person and contribute to integral human development."

The challenge lies not in whether to adopt AI technologies, but in how to do so in a manner that enhances rather than diminishes the human elements of education. This requires developing comprehensive ethical guidelines that address data privacy, algorithmic bias, and the preservation of human relationships in learning environments.

Abstract

This paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the Vatican's recent guidelines on artificial intelligence, examining their theological foundations, practical implications, and potential impact on Catholic institutions worldwide. Through systematic review and expert interviews, we identify key principles that should guide AI implementation in faith-based contexts.

1. Introduction

The rapid advancement of artificial intelligence technologies has prompted religious institutions worldwide to grapple with ethical, theological, and practical questions about their implementation and governance. The Vatican's response, articulated through various documents and papal addresses, provides a framework for understanding AI through the lens of Catholic social teaching.

2. Methodology

Our analysis employs a mixed-methods approach, combining document analysis of Vatican publications with semi-structured interviews of 25 Catholic educators and technologists. We utilized thematic analysis to identify recurring patterns and principles across these sources.
